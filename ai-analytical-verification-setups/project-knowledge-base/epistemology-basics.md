## 1. What is Knowledge?

Knowledge requires:
- A claim
- Evidence
- Justification

---

## 2. Levels of Evidence

- Proof (rare outside mathematics)
- Strong evidence
- Moderate evidence
- Weak evidence
- No evidence

---

## 3. Burden of Proof

- Lies with the person making the claim
- Extraordinary claims require stronger evidence

---

## 4. Uncertainty

- Absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence
- Some claims remain undecidable with current data

---

## 5. Bayesian Thinking (Simplified)

- Update beliefs based on new evidence
- Strong prior beliefs require strong evidence to change

---

## 6. Common Epistemic Errors

- Overconfidence
- Ignoring uncertainty
- Treating beliefs as facts
- Rejecting evidence without justification
